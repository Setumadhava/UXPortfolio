What Is ActiList?

ActiList is an app that connects people with similar interests and helps users get better recommendations on topics like movies and music from people who they know personally and share similar taste.

Users can create lists of their interests and add their activities into these lists. Users can follow a person or just a particular list of that person.

With ActiList people don’t have to worry about finding someone to discuss their unique taste in movies or books.

Create Lists of your interests

Books, Movies, Music or Travel. Create a list of anything that you care about and start adding activities into the lists as you do them.

Stay in touch

See what people like you are doing in the interest space. Learn about new activities that you know you will enjoy. You don’t have to regret watching that popular movie because it was rated highly by internet reviews.

Share your activities. Choose who can see.

Keep track of the interesting things you do.

You decide who gets to see the content. You can make private list or activity if you want to keep a personal list or share it with your followers.

Discover new interests

Search for activities or topics of your interest and discover activities by people on that topic. This is a great way to find people who like doing things you like to do.

Follow interesting people or lists

Follow interesting people and discover things they have posted. If you are only interested in a particular list of a person, follow only that list. Rest of the content from that person will not be shown in your feed.

Background

I have always enjoyed watching movies. In 2014 I started making a list of all the movies I watch so that I could get back to the list in the future and re-watch the movies I liked.

My main source of discovering new movies has been IMDB. Sometimes I would watch a great movie and other times I would feel like I wasted good 2 hours of my life even though a movie had great online ratings. When that started happening more often, I turned towards few friends whose recommendation I could always believe when it came to movie recommendations.

I started observing a gap in my tastes and the general public opinion. I then started wondering if there was a way to reduce this gap or come up with a new solution that addressed this issue. That’s when I felt the need of an app that let me find recommendation of topics I am interested in, be it movies or music or books, from people who share the same taste as I do and not depend on reviews posted in the internet from people I don't know.

Early Explorations

To validate my idea, I quickly came up with few skecthes describing the user flow and put them in-front of people. The goal of the sketches was only to validate the idea and not interaction patterns.

Research

While most people I spoke to agreed with what I was proposing, I wanted to hear from people who I didn't know personally. To gather that information, I ran a survey on online recommendations with 41 participants I hadn't interacted with before and analysed the data. Below are some important findings that helped me understand the users and problem better.

“I know my preferences and so do most of my friends so I trust their recommendations”

“Trust is the likely factor; friends or those close to me likely have same/similar tastes and preferences”

Persona & Scoping

The survey & the initial interviews helped me validate the idea and understand the needs of the users. To keep the project scoped and avoid feature-creep, I restrcited the idea around discovering & recommending movies.

Wireframes

The learnings of the research & the feedback I collected by showing the early prototype to people helped me inform the direction I need to be heading with the design. One of the most important learning was that people wanted the app to be 'List' centered that contained the activities they did.

I began the design process by sketching patterns and creating low-fidelity wireframes to define the interaction paths and navigational patterns. The wireframes went through a couple of rounds of iterations before they paved way for high-fidelity mock-ups.

I ran a set of quick usability tests with people around me and discovered areas of improvement. Some of them included:

  • Ability to subscribe to lists created by people apart from follwoing the people.
  • Apply multiple interest filters instead of one.
  • Privacy options for individual activity posts.

Visual Design

I started the visual design process by creating a style guide to help me with the design process. Once I had my styles and patterns ready, I designed the screens and the improved the interactions based on the above feedback.

High Fidelity Mockups

Once sure about the right user flow, interaction patterns and the visual guide for the app, I designed the high-fidelity mockups. The feedback from the usability testing were also incorporated.

Benefits

ActiList helps users stay better connected to their interests and also with people having similar interests.

ActiList’s user-curated lists helps users discover new and interesting activities to do and also connect with interesting people.

ActiList provides a faster and efficient way for users to gather opinion about a topic from people they know they can depend upon.

Finally, in the age of overwhelming content, ActiList helps us stay connected to interests & information we truly care about.

Reflections

This was my third detailed mobile interaction design project and I was quite pleased with the results I was able to achieve.

By keeping ‘Movies’ as my central theme, I was able to scope the project well and stay better focused on the story I was trying to design for.

I put in a lot more effort on visual design because my previous work hadn’t met my expectations. I was able to create a more detailed style guide and build the visual elements based off that.

I decided to design for the iOS because I had never designed an iPhone app before and I got a chance to explore and work on iOS’ design principles.

Beyond

In this exercise I designed around a movie enthusiast’s experince. However, there were a bunch of ideas I would like to explore.

The app has a lot of potential to expand into other spaces of interests and also e-commerce. For example, a restaurant might make a list of their menu which its patrons can subscribe to.

A clothes store can create lists of their collection and people can subscribe to a collection list they’re interested in.

In the future iterations I would like to explore content designs for commercial users and push the idea beyond the personal interest space.
