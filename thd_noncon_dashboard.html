Non-Conveyable Dashboard

An enterprise app that aims to improve the operational efficiency of outbound work assignment and tracking for the Supervisors working in Home Depot’s fulfillment centers.

ROLE

Product Designer

RESPONSIBILITIES

User Research, Usability Studies, Interaction & Product Design

TIMELINE

May - Jul 2019

The Problem

Customer order picking within the DFCs were managed by Outbound Supervisors using a refreshable spreadsheet. The spreadsheet tools were not built with any security and posed security risks for the organization.

Along with this, there was no consistency in the process followed by supervisors across different buildings or even within the same building and the tools did not provide the users visibility to critical information to make effective decisions.

The users were using a Spreadsheet-based tool for their job

How might we create a secure, centralized solution that allows the Supervisors to manage and track customer orders efficiently?

The Goal

The goal was to replace the existing spreadsheet-based tool with a cloud-based application that allowed users to carry out the same tasks with equal or better efficiency. No new features or process improvements was within the purview of the MVP.

Research

Due to security concerns associated with the spreadsheet tool, we had to design and deliver a replacement app within 3 months which gave me a short window to finish research & design. To make the best use of the limited time, I visited a Home Depot Fulfillment Center and did a Contextual Inquiry study to familiarize myself with the outbound process, the people doing the job and the problem they face.

Once I was familiar with the problem, the users’ journey and business goals, I facilitated a week-long design sprint at another Fulfillment Center. This sprint involved a developer, members from the business team and 2 users from different fulfillment centers.

During the sprint, I also conducted few Semi-Structured Interviews with the users to validate my findings from the Contextual Inquiry and understand the user-behaviors and problems in further detail.

Journey Maps

The Contextual Inquiry helped us understand the mental-model of the Supervisors and the various touch-points that exist in their job of managing customer orders.

The Journey Map for Work Assignment and Monitoring.

Insights

Some of the key findings on the current tool and process were:

Stressful

The lack of automation required a lot of repeated, redundant interactions with the tool throughout the day resulting in stress.

Less time spent with people

The supervisors wished they spent less time with the tool and more time with their associates building relationships and helping them be successful.

Slow and reactive

The tool did not provide users visibility to critical information when they needed. The users had to preemptively seek information.

Context switching is unproductive

The users used 3 different tools simultaneously in their order-management process resulting in constant context-switching.

“Hitting the Refresh button 6000 times a day is a pain in the a**."

"It's stressful as we get closer to the cut times because sometimes customer orders are not loaded and I have to do a lot of research to find and fix issues."

Defining The User

From the research learnings, I created a user-persona to consolidate the users’ needs, goals, process, pain-points and behaviors.

Persona of an Outbound Supervisor within a fulfillment center.

Ideation

Design Studio

I facilitated a design studio with the team to explore ideas to solve for the prioritized goals and user needs. During the process I tried to keep the conversations focused on the MVP and created a parking lot with a list of Nice-to-Haves.

The team explored a breadth of ideas to address the identified user needs & goals.

Wireframes

The Design Studio gave me a skeletal idea of the solution and I created digital wireframes too address the top user needs and goals.

The ideas explored, sketched, and prioritized during the Design Studio informed the first digital wireframe.

Usability & Iterations

We conducted a series of usability studies over the next couple of weeks to test our solution hypothesis and iterated based on the learnings.

Usability studies helped us validate our solution hypothesis and identify areas of concern or delight.

Final Design

From the study we learned that users cared more about specific research and not open-ended research as we initially believed. Based on this, I iterated on the designs to match their mental model more closely. The outcome was that users were able to take actions more easily and there was less ambiguity in what they could interact with and what they couldn’t.

A cleaner looking landing page that matches the users' mental models more closely.

Research section is moved from the front page. This offers a more focused view of the information users need.

Showing work by different cut times helps users prioritize the work accordingly.

Orders with exceptions are grouped together so that suitable action can be taken.

Outcomes

The efficiency of the Supervisors and their decisions directly impacts The Home Depot's online shopping experience and the tools they use directly impacts the building's efficiency. As a result, it was very important for us to make sure we provide the Supervisors a tool that makes them feel confident using while improving their efficiency and security. And we were able to deliver that in 10 weeks, well ahead of our schedule.

When we tested the final version of the app, the users felt comfortable and confident using the new app. They were able run the operations smothly with very little to no training needed and this was a huge win for us.

“I love that I don’t have to use the concatenator tool anymore”

“The app is pretty self-explanatory.”

Few aspects of the project, like remote usability studies, could've gone better. Nonetheless, these issues didn't derail the project and for the most part I managed to stay focused on the MVP and included all stakeholders in the entire design process which made the project a success.

This project gave me a lot of confidence in runnings design sprints, making big design decisions and situational adaptability.
